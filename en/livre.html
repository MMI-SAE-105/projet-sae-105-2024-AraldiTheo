Money timeline

Book : L’incroyable histoire de l’argent

"The Incredible History of Money" by Daniel Confland explores in depth the evolution of money from its origins to the present day, highlighting its impact on human societies and the economy. The book traces the history of money, showing how this simple tool of exchange revolutionized economic and social interactions.

Balance temps et argent

Money, as we know it, has not always existed. Early societies operated through barter, a system of direct exchange of goods and services. However, barter had significant limitations: it was difficult to determine the value of goods, and exchanges often required a double coincidence of needs (two people having to own goods that they needed each other). It was this inefficiency that led to the emergence of money.

Troc balance

The earliest forms of money were rare and valuable objects such as shells, gemstones, and livestock. Gradually, metals, especially gold and silver, were adopted because of their durability, rarity, and malleability. One of the great monetary innovations came with the introduction of the first coins in Lydia in the 7th century BC, which marked the beginning of the widespread use of metallic currency.

Over time, monetary systems became more complex. With the rise of great civilizations, such as the Roman Empire or ancient Greece, money became an essential factor in the economy, facilitating large-scale trade and allowing the emergence of a more organized economy. In the Middle Ages, banks began to develop, especially in Italy, with the first forms of loans, deposits and transfers of funds.

Anciennes pièces

The advent of banknotes in the modern period marked another milestone in the evolution of money. First introduced in China in the 9th century, they spread to Europe in the 17th and 18th centuries, with the founding of central banks such as the Bank of England in 1694. Banknotes allowed promises of payment to be exchanged for goods, making trade more convenient and secure.

Today, the evolution of money continues with the digitization of transactions and the rise of cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin. These new forms of currency, independent of central banks, raise numerous questions about the future of the global monetary system, paving the way for new transformations in the economy.
